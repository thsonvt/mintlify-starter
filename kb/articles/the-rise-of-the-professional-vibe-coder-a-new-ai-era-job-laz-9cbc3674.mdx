## My biggest takeaways from this conversation:

1. **Vibe coding is now a full-time professional career.** Lazar Jovanovic gets paid to build internal tools and public-facing products using AI tools like Lovable. His work ranges from Shopify integrations and merch stores that handle real transactions to complex internal dashboards tracking feature adoption. Companies across the S&P 500 are actively hiring people with these skills.
2. **Not having a technical background can be an advantage when vibe coding.** People without engineering experience donâ€™t know whatâ€™s â€œsupposedly impossible,â€ so they try things that technical people might dismiss. 4. **AI tools have a limited context window that you must manage deliberately.** Think of it like a genie granting you three wishes. Every request consumes tokens for reading, thinking, and executing. If you dump vague requests, the AI spends the most tokens figuring out what you want and has little left for quality output.
5. **Start five parallel builds to find clarity faster.** Instead of overthinking one design, brain dump into the first project, get more specific in the second, add visual references in the third, attach actual code snippets in the fourth, and compare all five. This costs more up front but saves hundreds of credits and days of iteration later.
6. **Create four PRD files before serious building begins:** master plan (what and why), implementation plan (sequence of work), design guidelines (visual parameters), and user journeys (how people navigate). These feed into a tasks.md file that breaks everything into executable steps. The agent reads these files before each prompt.
7. **When you get stuck, follow this four-step debugging sequence.** First, let the tool try to fix it. Second, add console logs to track whatâ€™s happening and paste the output back to the agent. Third, bring in an external tool like Codex for deeper diagnosis. Fourth, revert a few steps and try a cleaner prompt.
8. **Use rules files to make your findings permanent.** After solving a problem, ask the AI how to prompt it better next time, and then add that guidance to your rules.md file. The agent reads these rules on every request, so youâ€™re training it to handle your weaknesses automatically.
9. **Design and taste are becoming increasingly valuable in the AI era.** When everyone can produce good-enough output instantly, the people who stand out are those who understand emotional design decisionsâ€”fonts, spacing, micro-interactionsâ€”that AI canâ€™t yet replicate well. Invest time in studying elite design work.
10. **Build in public to land vibe-coding jobs.** Lazar got hired at Lovable because he was already shipping projects, teaching on YouTube, and sharing knowledge on LinkedIn.
