## Summary

Tom MacWright critiques the rise of LLM-generated job applications, emphasizing their impersonal nature and lack of genuine self-representation.

## Key Insights

> "The perfected, generated, prompted resume is generic and impersonal."
>
> — Tom MacWright's observation on the nature of LLM-generated resumes.

> "They haven't put themselves out there. They haven't said anything true."
>
> — MacWright's critique of the authenticity of individuals using LLMs for job applications.

## Full Article

24th June 2026 In the last few months, I've started to see [job applications] that were clearly cowritten by an LLM, link to an LLM-generated portfolio site, which then links to LLM-generated GitHub projects, with purely LLM-generated commit messages. [...] My other reaction is that I don't know anything about these people. They haven't put themselves out there. They haven't said anything true. [...] The perfected, generated, prompted resume is generic and impersonal. It tells me nothing about this person, other than that they use particular tools. — Tom MacWright, Accidental anonymity
